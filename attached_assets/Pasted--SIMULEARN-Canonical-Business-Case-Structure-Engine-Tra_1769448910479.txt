





SIMULEARN
Canonical Business Case Structure
(Engine Training & Case Authoring Packet – POC v1)












PURPOSE OF THIS DOCUMENT
This document defines the canonical structure for all SimuLearn business cases used in the February POC.
Its purpose is to ensure that:
All cases feel academically legitimate
All cases feel real-world and immersive
The AI engine behaves consistently and safely
Professors maintain full pedagogical control
Students engage in applied reasoning, not answer-hunting
This structure is mandatory for POC cases.

OVERALL CASE CONSTRAINTS (LOCKED)
Target discipline: Business
Target level: Undergraduate
Case duration: 20–25 minutes total
Decision points: 3 (max 4 only if explicitly justified)
Language: Spanish
Assessment status: Non-graded (POC only)
Primary objective:
Flow & completion
Authentic decision-making experience
⚠️ Depth of decision quality is secondary in the POC, not ignored.

SECTION 1 — CASE CONTEXT (NARRATIVE SETUP)
Objective
Place the student inside a realistic business situation with enough context to make decisions meaningful — without turning the case into an essay or story.
Rules (LOCKED)
Length: 120–180 words
Tone: Professional, neutral, real-world
Narrative is functional, not literary
Every sentence must support decision-making
Must Include
Organization type (company, startup, division, etc.)
Student role (explicit authority level)
Current situation or pressure
Time constraint or urgency
Clear reason why decisions matter now
Must NOT Include
Emotional storytelling
Character arcs
Historical overviews
Academic theory explanations
Hidden “correct” clues
Engine Instruction
Narrative context is permitted only to establish realism and immersion.
 It must never introduce information that does not directly affect decisions.

SECTION 2 — CORE BUSINESS CHALLENGE
Objective
Define the central problem the organization is facing.
Rules (LOCKED)
Single primary challenge
Clearly constrained (budget, time, resources, uncertainty)
No jargon-heavy framing
No implied “correct” direction
Must Include
What is at risk
What cannot be changed
What is uncertain
What success might look like (without defining it)
Engine Instruction
The problem statement frames tension, not solutions.

SECTION 3 — DECISION POINT 1 (ORIENTATION DECISION)
Objective
Introduce the student to the simulation mechanics while establishing agency.
Format (LOCKED FOR POC)
Multiple choice
3–4 options
Each option represents a strategic posture, not a solution
Critical Rule (VERY IMPORTANT)
There is no correct or incorrect option.
Each option must:
Be defensible
Have logical rationale
Lead to different downstream consequences
Example Framing (Conceptual)
Option A: Prioritize short-term stability
Option B: Invest in long-term capability
Option C: Balance risk conservatively
Engine Instruction
Multiple-choice options must never be evaluated as right or wrong — only as directional paths.

SECTION 4 — CONSEQUENCES & FEEDBACK (AFTER DECISION 1)
Objective
Show the impact of the decision in a realistic, non-judgmental way.
Consequence Types (LOCKED)
Narrative consequence
What happens next in the organization
System indicators (visual or textual)
Budget (↑ / ↓ / stable)
Team morale
Operational risk
Strategic flexibility
⚠️ Every decision must change at least one indicator negatively (opportunity cost).
Tone Rules
Encouraging
Mentorship-oriented
Never evaluative
Never corrective
Prohibited Language
“Correct”
“Wrong”
“Best”
“Optimal”
“You should have…”

SECTION 5 — DECISION POINT 2 (ANALYTICAL DECISION)
Objective
Move from orientation into reasoned judgment.
Format (LOCKED)
Short written justification (5–7 lines)
Open-ended
No word-count pressure
Prompt Design Rules
Ask how and why
Never ask what is the right answer
Encourage consideration of trade-offs
Engine Behavior
If response is weak or vague:
Ask clarifying questions
Highlight missing considerations
Offer alternative perspectives (not answers)

SECTION 6 — CONSEQUENCES & FEEDBACK (AFTER DECISION 2)
Objective
Deepen realism and complexity.
Required Elements
Compounded effects from Decision 1
Stakeholder reactions (management, team, customers, etc.)
Emerging tensions or unintended consequences
Ethics Rule (LOCKED)
Ethics must emerge implicitly, never as a direct question.

SECTION 7 — DECISION POINT 3 (INTEGRATIVE DECISION)
Objective
Force synthesis of:
Information
Trade-offs
Prior consequences
Format (LOCKED)
Short written justification
Focus on integration, not creativity for its own sake
Design Constraint
Decision must feel consequential
No “perfect balance” outcomes
Realistic ambiguity is encouraged

SECTION 8 — FINAL OUTCOME & RESOLUTION
Objective
Provide closure and meaning, not judgment.
Must Include
Trajectory summary
Where the organization stands now
Sense of accomplishment
Something was achieved
Progress occurred
Open-ended future
Trade-offs remain
Real life continues
Tone
Respectful
Grounded
Encouraging
Never graded
Engine Instruction
The final outcome must acknowledge effort and impact without evaluating correctness or performance.

SECTION 9 — REFLECTION (LIGHTWEIGHT)
Objective
Encourage metacognition without burden.
Format (LOCKED)
1 optional prompt
Example:
“What factor most influenced your decisions?”
“What would you explore differently next time?”
⚠️ No long reflections. No essays.

HARD PROHIBITIONS (ENGINE SAFETY RULES)
The engine must never:
Reveal a “correct” answer
Optimize for GPA or grades
Mimic professor preferences
Respond with profanity
Engage in inappropriate humor
Mirror student misuse or bad behavior
The engine must always:
Maintain academic tone
Redirect inappropriate input professionally
Preserve institutional trust

DATA & MEMORY RULES (POC)
Student identity may be stored (name)
Decision path may be stored
Exact textual answers should not be reused
No cross-student leakage
No long-term profiling (POC)
